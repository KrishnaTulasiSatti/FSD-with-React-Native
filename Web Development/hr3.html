Understanding Your Condition

The **common cold**, a highly contagious respiratory illness, is primarily caused by various influenza viruses. It spreads efficiently from person to person through respiratory droplets released during coughing, sneezing, or even talking.

Symptoms typically manifest as a runny or stuffy nose, sore throat, cough, and occasionally a mild fever. While generally self-limiting, understanding its nature helps in managing symptoms and preventing further spread.

Learn More About the Common Cold

Recommended Care Pathway

Diagnostic Tests: Given the typical presentation of a common cold, specific diagnostic tests are generally not required.

Observation Period: Kindly monitor your symptoms closely over the next 24 to 48 hours. Note any changes or new developments.

Professional Consultation: Should your symptoms intensify, persist for an extended period, or if you experience any concerning new symptoms, it is highly advisable to consult a medical professional promptly.
