Understanding the Common Cold

The **common cold** is a highly contagious viral infection of the nose and throat. It is caused by many different types of viruses, with rhinoviruses being the most common. It spreads easily through airborne droplets from coughs and sneezes, or by direct contact with contaminated surfaces.

Symptoms typically appear one to three days after exposure and can include a runny or stuffy nose, sore throat, cough, congestion, slight body aches or a mild headache, and sometimes sneezing. The common cold is usually harmless and symptoms typically resolve within 7 to 10 days.

Learn More on Wikipedia

Recommended Care & Next Steps

  • Rest: Ensure adequate rest to help your body recover.
  • Hydration: Drink plenty of fluids like water, juice, and broth.
  • Medical Consultation: If symptoms worsen, persist beyond 10 days, or new severe symptoms (e.g., difficulty breathing, chest pain) develop, please **consult a doctor promptly.**
  • Prevention: Wash hands frequently, avoid touching your face, and cover coughs/sneezes.
